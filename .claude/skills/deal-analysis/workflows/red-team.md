<process>

## Step 1: Load Context

Ensure CIM or company information is loaded. If a memo has already been generated in this conversation, use that as additional context — the red team should specifically challenge claims made in the memo.

If nothing is loaded: "What deal are we red-teaming? Share the CIM or tell me about the company."

## Step 2: Adopt Adversarial Personas

Analyze the deal from three distinct perspectives:

**Persona 1: The Skeptical LP**
"Why should I put money behind this? What's the downside case?"
- Focus: returns assumptions, market risk, execution complexity, capital at risk
- Question every growth projection and multiple expansion assumption
- Challenge whether the base case is actually conservative or just dressed up as one

**Persona 2: The Operating Partner**
"What's going to go wrong in year one? What are you not seeing from the outside?"
- Focus: operational complexity, management gaps, integration risk, customer churn post-transition
- Challenge the "keep doing what you're doing" thesis — what actually changes when the owner leaves?
- Look for hidden dependencies on the current owner's relationships, expertise, or reputation

**Persona 3: The Industry Insider**
"You don't understand this market as well as you think."
- Focus: competitive dynamics, market timing, technology disruption, regulatory risk
- Challenge industry growth assumptions — are the tailwinds real or narrative?
- Identify what competitors or market shifts could erode the value proposition

## Step 3: Deliver the Red Team

For each persona, present:

1. **The core concern** — 1-2 sentences, stated bluntly
2. **Supporting evidence** — What in the CIM or available info supports this concern? Cite pages where possible.
3. **The question you should be asking** — A specific diligence question to resolve this concern
4. **What would change my mind** — What evidence or answer would mitigate this concern

## Step 4: Synthesize

After the three perspectives, provide:

- **The single biggest reason to walk away** — if there is a deal-killer, name it plainly
- **The single biggest reason the concerns are manageable** — the bull case for why these risks are acceptable
- **Three questions for the management meeting** — the three specific questions that would most reduce uncertainty about this deal

Be direct. If the deal has a real problem, say so. If the concerns are manageable, say that too. Don't hedge everything into mush.

</process>

<success_criteria>
This workflow is complete when:
- [ ] Three distinct adversarial perspectives presented
- [ ] Each grounded in available evidence with citations
- [ ] Specific diligence questions generated for each concern
- [ ] Mitigation criteria stated for each concern
- [ ] Synthesis: walk-away vs. proceed assessment delivered
- [ ] Three management questions provided
</success_criteria>
