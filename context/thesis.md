# Wavelength Equity — Investment Thesis

## Fund Profile

Wavelength Equity is a traditional search fund acquiring one platform company in the lower middle market. The operator (Dino) steps in as CEO — "chapter 2 leadership for an exceptional company." Based in New York, prior operating experience in offshore IT services.

## Target Company

- **Revenue:** $5M–$65M. Will consider $2–3M for high-growth industries with sticky revenues (e.g., cybersecurity, vertical SaaS) — rationale: company grows into target size within the first year.
- **EBITDA:** $1M+ floor. Flexible downward for fast-growing companies with recurring revenue.
- **Revenue quality:** Sticky revenues, ideally contractual recurring or subscription-based.
- **Growth:** Both industry and company growth required. Key indicators: LTM YoY revenue growth and 3-year CAGR.
- **Capital efficiency:** Low capital intensity, strong margins.
- **Geography:** USA only. NYC metro area receives modest positive bias.
- **Funding:** No prior investor funding. Bootstrapped only.

## Ideal Owner Profile

- Bootstrapped owner-operator, typically 45–70 years old, seeking to slow down or retire.
- Led the company for 10–20+ years and brought it to maturity.
- Cares deeply about team, customers, and legacy.
- Wavelength's value prop: competitive offer (liquidity event) bundled with an operator who carries the company forward.
- **Red flags:** Young/energetic owner who is the obvious choice to keep running the business — they need a capital partner, not an operator. Wavelength's bid won't stand out. Also skeptical of companies founded 2–3 years ago that aren't yet proven.

## Primary Industry Focus

**Specialized MSSPs/MSPs** with two sub-theses:

**(A) Vertical specialization** — Managed IT for specific verticals (dental IT, hospitality, etc.). More defensible than generalist MSPs due to industry-specific hardware/software expertise and switching costs.

**(B) OT security** — Operational Technology security protecting hardware/software that monitors and controls physical devices (ICS, SCADA, IoT) across manufacturing, energy, and utilities.

Rationale: small, defensible niches. Action plan: significant resource investment (conferences, river guides, deep outreach).

## Secondary Industries

Automated high-volume outreach, no conference-level investment:

- **Penetration testing** — 12% overall growth, 29% for PTaaS. Challenge: commoditization, moderately prone to agentic AI disruption.
- **SOC (Security Operations Centers)** — general.
- **Fire safety** — attractive ICT business model. Challenge: picked over by PE. Backing off.
- **Technical education / trade schools** — thesis: find another deal resembling Fire Tech Productions.
- **Vertical SaaS** — inspection management, construction technology.
- **PEOs (Professional Employer Organizations)**

Will also opportunistically consider opportunities outside target industries.

## Risk Factors to Avoid

- Customer concentration (>20% single customer, >50% top 3, >75% top 5)
- Declining overall market
- Early-stage / unproven business models
- Owners who seem like the obvious choice to keep running the business
